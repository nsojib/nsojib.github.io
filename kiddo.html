Kiddo: Robot as an educational tool

Why this robot? Children are easily attracted to technology. Incorporating exciting technology for example a robot can play an important role in their education. Our goal is to develop a friendly robot such that young children feel comfortable around it and interacting with it to enhance their learning.
The Robot: The name of this robot is Kiddo. It consists of a 3DOF arm, an android phone, a whiteboard, two buttons, and a driving circuit.
Current Research: Currently, a student is working with this robot with the title "learning from demonstration". In this experiment the task is a kid will draw any shape on the whiteboard then the robot will recognize the shape, generate trajectory if the shape is unknown otherwise use a pre-trained trajectory to draw the shape. We believe interacting this way will give the child a degree of commandability while kids love to give commands and eventually keep him/her engage in learning.


Simulation First, we have designed the robot in VREP and then simulated it. This simulation version is similar to the physical robot and helps us to implement algorithms before applying to the real robot.
