Single Cell Braille Book Reader

While digital braille display is a costly one and making is also very complex, Prof. Dr. M. Zafar Iqbal took an initiative to make a simple braille book reader for visually impaired peoples and I worked with him in this project.

The Single Cell Reading Idea On a braille book, a visually-impaired person moves his/her finger from left to right over all the consecutive cells and discern what text it is. Our idea is, the same feelings can be generated if the user puts his/her finger rest over the cell and the cell moves from right to left.
How a braille cell represent text
To represent Bengali character ‘আ’ dot 1,2,6 are in down state and Dot 3,4,5 in raise up state shown in the figure above.
Our Device and Experiment
A single braille cell is really small and contains 6 dots where each dot needs to individually raise up/ down to represent a character. The video shows a demo while Afsana, a visually impaired student of SUST was trying to read the book “Meyetir nam narina” through the reader.
